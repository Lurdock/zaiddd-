Seagulls (Gulls) — quick guide

Facts, behavior, and simple tips for coexisting with gulls on coasts and in cities.

About seagulls

"Seagull" is a common name for many gull species in the family Laridae. Gulls are adaptable, opportunistic birds often found near water and human settlements.

Family
Laridae
Diet
Omnivore/scavenger
Range
Worldwide (coasts, inland)

Key behaviors

  • Social: nest in colonies and often feed in groups.
  • Intelligent: use tools, drop shellfish on rocks to break them, learn to follow humans for food.
  • Vocal: loud, distinctive calls used for communication and alarm.

Quick tips for coexistence

  • Avoid leaving food out: secure bins and pack meals away promptly.
  • Do not feed gulls deliberately — it encourages bold behavior and dependency.
  • Protect eggs and nests: give nesting colonies space during breeding season.

Conservation

Some gulls thrive near humans; others are declining due to habitat loss and changing fisheries. Support local conservation programs and avoid disturbing nesting sites.

Identification tips

Look at size, bill shape, leg color, and wing pattern. Juveniles often look very different from adults.
